A small satellite is to be launched into orbit using a rocket. A truss structure is used to hold the satellite inside the rocket during launch. The satellite is idealised as a point mass $m$, held by four massless trusses of length $l$ and axial stiffness $EA$, as shown in Figure 1. The stallite has two degrees of freedom, $r_1$ and $r_2$.


Text for question 2.





Solution

Using the unit displacement method:

Consider $r_1=1$ and $r_2=0$:

The bar forces are:

$N_{12}=\frac{EA}{l}, \; N_{13}=\frac{EA}{\sqrt{2}l}, \; N_{14}=-\frac{EA}{\sqrt{2}l}, \; N_{12}=-\frac{EA}{l}$

Equilibrium of the horizontal and vertical forces leads to:

$R_1=(2+\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2})\frac{EA}{l}=3, \; R_2=0$

This is the first column of the stiffness matrix.

Now consider $r_1=0$ and $r_2=1$:

The bar forces are:

$N_{12}=N_{15}=0, \; N_{13}=N_{14}=-\frac{EA}{\sqrt{2}l}$

Equilibrium of the horizontal and vertial forces leads to:

$R_1=0, \; R_2=\frac{EA}{l}$

This is the second column of the stiffness matrix.

Thus, the stiffness matric is:

$\textbf{$K$}= \frac{EA}{l} \begin{bmatrix}3&0\\0&1\end{bmatrix}$
